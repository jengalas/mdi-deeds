   Date: 5 February 1877
   Town: Bar Harbor
   Grantors: Charles Willing and Chapman Biddle, trustees of the estate formerly of William Bingham the elder
   Grantee: Lucy Ellis
   Description:
      Beginning at the sea an[d] the east line of the Dorr Lot, so-called, which lot was part of the allotment of the lands of the Bingham estate in said town of Eden;
      then running along the said east line of the said Dorr Lot S 19° W through a fir tree and skirting the west side of the ravine to the upland, and continuing the same course 100 perches from the said fir tree to a stake and stone near a crotched fir tree; so far bounded northwesterly by said east line of said Dorr lot;
      then running N 85 ° E 82 perches to a birch stake and stones on the brink of the cliff;
      then continuing the same course to the sea; bounded from the first herein mentioned stake and stone to said sea southeasterly by land formerly of said William Bingham now held in trust by said Willing and Biddle as said trustees;
      and then running by the sea by the several courses and distances thereof, in the main northwesterly and bounded by the sea to the place of beginning.
      And appurtenant to the said parcel of land hereby conveyed, a right of way 2 rods wide for animals, vehicles, and on foot and for all purposes from said parcel of land southerly and westerly and to the same easterly and northerly over and through the land of the grantors to and from the highway from Bar Harbor to Schooner Head in said town of Eden by the easiest feasible route to be located whenever the grantors or their heirs or assigns or the grantee and her heirs and assigns shall agree or if they do not agree, whenever the County Commissioners of said County of Hancock shall determine.

737:317–318
   Date: 24 October 1950
   Town: Bar Harbor
   Grantors: Evelyn Pierrepont Luquer and City Bank Farmers Trust Company, executors and trustees under Last Will and Testament of Anne Low Pierrepont Luquer
   Grantees: George S. Munson and Katharine T. Munson (husband and wife)
   Description: land and buildings known as "Eagle Cliff"; 20 acres, more or less
      Beginning at low water mark on Frenchman Bay at the northeast corner of the Dorr lot, so-called, being land of heirs of C. H. Dorr, deceased, which lot was part of the allotment of the lands of the Bingham Estate in said town of Eden;
      then running, following and bounded northwesterly by the easterly line of said Dorr lot;
      southwesterly by the easterly line of said Dorr lot;
      southwesterly to an iron bolt set or to be forthwith set in the ground at high water mark;
      then S 19°30′ W, but always following and bounded northwesterly by said easterly line of said Dorr lot 83 rods and 9 links to an iron bolt set or to be forthwith set in the ledge in said line;
      then N 85°30′ E passing through an iron bolt set or to be forthwith set in the ledge at the top of a high bluff near the shore 75 rods and 6 links bounded southerly by other land of me the said grantor not hereby conveyed or released but hereinafter excepted and reserved, to high water mark;
      then on the same course still bounded southerly b other land of said grantor not hereby conveyed or released but hereinafter excepted and reserved to low water mark;
      then following and bounded northeasterly by low water mark by its various courses and distances, mainly, northwesterly to the first herein mentioned corner at the place of beginning.
      Excepting, however, from the above described lot of land, a certain lot or parcel of land deeded by Anne Pierrepont Luquer to George B. Dorr by 432:456 (20 September 1906) described in said deed as follows, to wit:
         Beginning at the corner of land of Harvard University and said Anne Pierrepont Luquer on line of land of said George B. Dorr marked by stone post and iron bolt;
         then N 21°15′ E (old course N 19°30′ E) 447 feet following boundary line between said Dorr and Luquer to a stone post set in the ground on said line and at southerly and easterly line of right of way granted by C. H. Dorr to Lucy Ellis;
         then on said course 15 feet to the center line of said right of way;
         then S 24°30′ E along center line of right of way granted by Lucy Ellis to Matilda C. Markoe, 22.3 feet to a point 16.5 feet distant from last mentioned stone post, and at right angle to said Dorr and Luquer line;
         then S 21°15′ W 99.4 feet along center line of said last mentioned right of way to a point 16.5 feet distant from a stone post set in the ground, and at right angle to said Dorr and Luquer line;
         then S 5°30′ E 81.2 feet along center line of said right of way to a point 16.5 feet distant from and at right angle to the last described line to a stone post set in the ground;
         then S 20°40′ E 77.8 feet along the center line of said right of way to a point 16.5 feet distant from and at right angle to the last above described line to a stone post set in the ground;
         then S 3°55′ W 108.5 feet along the center line of said right of way to a point 16.5 feet distant from and at right angle to the last described line, to a stone post set in the ground;
         then S 2°45′ E 54.3 feet along the center line of said right of way to intersection with boundary line between said Harvard University land and land of Anne Pierrepont Luquer;
         then S 87°15′ W (old course S 85°30′ W) 16.5 feet to a stone post set in the ground on said boundary line for the western boundary of said right of way;
         then on same course S 87°15′ W 153 feet to place of beginning, containing 29,059 square feet, more or less.
