   Date: 25 April 1881

      then along the said line of lot #51 and the southeast line of lot #48, also conveyed to Richard Higgins aforesaid, N 45° E to the place of beginning.

501:217–219
   Date: 17 September 1913
   Town: Bar Harbor
   Grantor: Lena M. Lynam
   Grantee: Bar Harbor Water Company
   Description: Two lots bordering east shore of Eagle Lake.
      Lot one:
         Beginning at a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground on the eastern shore of Eagle Lake at the southwesterly corner of land of Charles E. Lindall;
         then S 38° 25′ E, but always following said land of said Lindall to a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground in the westerly side of the old private way leading from the Eagle Lake Road to the camp of William M. Roberts;
         then following said westerly side of said old way southerly to a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground at a point 25 feet distant from the line next above described to a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground at a point 25 feet distant from the line next above described, measuring at right angles from said line;
         then westerly parallel with and everywhere 25 feet distant from the line first hereinabove described to a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground on the eastern shore of Eagle Lake;
         then following said shore to the place of beginning. [subject to "the exception and reservation" in deed (448:437; 4 May 1908) from William M. Roberts to Lena M. Lynam; namely, the right to "widen said private way not exceeding thirty feet"]
      Lot two:
         Beginning at a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground on the eastern shore of Eagle Lake at the southwesterly corner of the lot first hereinabove described;
         then S 38° 25′ E, but always following said lot first hereinabove described to a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground in the westerly side of the old private way leading from the Eagle Lake Road to the camp of said Roberts, said iron pipe marking the southeasterly corner bound of said lot first hereinabove described;
         then N 52° W to a piece of iron pipe driven in the ground on the eastern shore of Eagle Lake;
         then following said shore in a northerly direction to the place of beginning.
