The deed found in book 2 on pages 419–420 covered two parcels on the Peters Plan: C30 (back lot) and G10 (first lot). This web page follows transactions involving the back lot.


2:419–420
   Date: 28 March 1792
   Town: Bar Harbor
   Grantors: Bartholomy DeGregoire and Maria Theresa DeGregoire
   Grantee: John Cousins
   Description: 100 acres, composed of 2 lots:
      first lot (65 acres, 108 [square] rods):
         Beginning at a birch tree, the northwest bound of said [John] Cousins' lot;
         run first S 38° W 126 rods to a poplar tree;
         then S 56.25[°] E 100 rods to a maple tree;
         then N 26° E 132 rods to the shore;
         then following the shore to the first bound.
      back lot (34 acres):
         Begin ... at the north corner of Elisha Cousins' lot;
         run first N 56.25° W 51 rods to a spruce tree, the bound between him [John Cousins] and Monsieur DeGregoire;
         then S 33.75° W 112 rods to Mr. Smallidge's line;
         then S 60.25° E 51 rods;
         then N 33.75° E to the first mentioned bound.
   Note: The directions and distances in the back lot do not lead to an ending point that is coincident with the beginning point.
   Grantors obtained this parcel via: grant from the General Court of Massachusetts
   Grantee disposed of the back lot of this parcel via 25:133

   Grantee disposed of this parcel via 33:159
