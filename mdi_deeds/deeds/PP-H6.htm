474:475–479
   Date: 6 October 1910
   Town: Bar Harbor
   Grantors: Samuel W. Bates, Frank C. Bates (named to administer the will of Charles T. How after Waldron Bates, the executor named in How's will, died)
   Grantee: Rudolph Brunnow
   Description: 12.33 acres, more or less, "exclusive of shore between high and low water mark"; This parcel was owned jointly by Charles T. How, and Warren Delano and Frederic D. Hitch. This deed conveyed How's share.
         Beginning at an iron bolt set in the ground in the eastern line of the Schooner Head Road and in the northern line of land of William H. Bliss;
         then N 5° E, but following said Schooner Head Road, 572 feet;
         then N 30′ E, but still following said Schooner Head Road, 327 feet to the Meadow Brook;
         then following said Meadow Brook by its various courses, but generally northerly and easterly, to its mouth at low water mark of Frenchman Bay (said Meadow Brook being the boundary between the herein conveyed lot on the south and land of estate of William Bingham on the north);
         then by line of low water mark by its various courses, but generally southerly, about 1200 feet to the northern line of shore or flats of William H. Bliss;
         then westerly, but everywhere following the north line of shore or flats of William H. Bliss, to high water mark;
         then N 83° W, but everywhere following the north line of land of William H. Bliss, 460 feet to the point of beginning.

592:147–154
   Date: 6 October 1910
   Town: Bar Harbor
   Grantors: Thomas T. Sherman, Edward P. Beckwith, Charles Elliot Warren (executors and trustees of the last will and testament of Rudolph Brunnow)
   Grantee: Eva van Cortlandt Hawkes
   Description: 12.33 acres, more or less, "exclusive of the shore between high and low water mark"
         Beginning at a point in the easterly line of the Schooner Head Road and in the northerly line of land now or late of William H. Bliss, which point is marked by an iron bolt set in the ground;
         and running from there N 5° E, and following and along the said Schooner Head Road, 572 feet;
         then N 30′ E, still along and following the said Schooner Head Road, 327 feet to the Meadow Brook;
         then following said Meadow Brook by its various courses, but generally northerly and easterly, to its mouth at low water mark of Frenchman Bay (said Meadow Brook being the boundary between the herein-described and conveyed land on the south and land formerly belonging to William Bingham, deceased, on the north);
         then by and along the line of low water mark by its various courses, but generally southerly, about 1200 feet to the northerly line of shore or flats now or formerly of William H. Bliss;
         then westerly, but everywhere following the north line of shore or flats now or formerly of William H. Bliss, to high water mark;
         then N 83° W, but everywhere following the northerly line of said land now or formerly of William H. Bliss, 460 feet to the point or place of beginning.
   Grantor (Rudolph Brunnow via his estate) obtained this parcel via 475–479 and 474:480–483

595:463–464
   Date: 29 July 1925

   Grantee: Eva van Cortlandt Hawkes
   Description: 3.77 acres, more or less; This parcel now contains Low Seas
         Beginning at a stone post in or near the east side of the Schooner Head Road, so-called;
         then S 84° E 29.36 rods, more or less, passing through an iron bolt about 4 rods, more or less, from the shore of Frenchman Bay, to the shore of said Frenchman Bay at high water mark;
         then continuing the same course, to wit, S 84° E across the said shore of Frenchman Bay to the line of low water mark on said Frenchman Bay;
         then generally southerly, but following said line of said low watermark of said Frenchman Bay, 17.32 rods, more or less, but to Meadow Brook, so-called, the north line of other land of said Eva Van Cortland Hawkes;
         then generally westerly and southwesterly, but following said Meadow Brook, the said north line of said other land of said Eva van Cortland Hawkes, to the center of said Schooner Head Road;
         then generally northerly, but following the center of said Schooner Head Road, 36.24 rods, more or less, to a point bearing N 84° W from the stone post, the point of beginning;
         then S 84° E to said stone post, the place of beginning.
